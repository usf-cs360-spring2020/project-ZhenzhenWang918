Worldwide Incidence of COVID-19

Encoding

I chose to use a map, which is the obvious way to show people how bad it is right now. Many maps of the pandemic have been made, but I want to incorporate the best features from each and add some of my own to tell the most accurate story possible.

The world map encodes each country's confirmed cases and deaths for a particular date, shaded on a log scale.

The graphs below show the new daily cases (averaged over seven days) for each country.

Interactivity: How to use this visualization

  • Move mouse over the graphs at the bottom to move the map's date forward or backwards.
  • Hover over a country to see total confirmed cases and deaths on that day.
  • Click on a country to highlight its curve in the graph.

Findings

  • As of May 2020, it is obvious that the war against COVID-19 is not over. The total number COVID-19 cases worldwide is still increasing very fast and shifting to new hotspots.
  • In countries that are currently doing the worst, such as Brazil, Russia, and the US, have done very little politically to respond to the pandemic. They appeared to have slow growth at the beginning only due to the fact there was little testing, and now they have no choice but to test more people and have their confirmed cases rise very quickly.
  • In some of the worst-hit places the number of daily infections has peaked, while other countries that avoided the first waves are just beginning to tick up.
  • A few areas such as Taiwan have done especially well at controlling the spread throughout the whole crisis. Some, like Singapore, kept numbers under control initially but had an outbreak later that forced it into a more aggressive lockdown. Others, like South Korea, saw one of the earliest major outbreaks (outside China) but have successfully controlled it since then.
  • Given that we are also seeing second waves of cases, which means we should be err on the side of caution when reopening.
